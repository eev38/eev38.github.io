## Population genomic structure of sorghum landraces across landscape, environment and culture

Sorghum is a staple crop consumed by billions of people globally. We take an integrative approach in our assesement of sorghum population structure by modeling gene flow in the context of both geographic space and human movement (travel time). We also consider the degree to which climate,space and language explain genetic diversity and shape the pattern of geographic structuring. By utilizing identity by descent segments, we assess spatiotemporal differences in the degree of shared ancestry and pattern of genetic relatedness across space, which has not been widely implemented in crop landscape genetics. We also develop a novel implementation of pairwise haplotype sharing with redundancy analysis (RDA) to determine how the importance of different factors explaining geographic population structure has changed over time.
