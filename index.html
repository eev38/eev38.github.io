I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Plant Biology program at Penn. State University (Lasky lab). My research integrates complex molecular, phenotypic and ecological data to understand how genetic variation is maintained in sorghum landraces and natural Arabidopsis thaliana populations.


With extensive experience using R, Python, and bash my technical skills range from predictive modeling to machine learning in HPC environments.


I am passionate about the diverse applications of data science and supercomputing, particularly in the context of agriculture. Outside of my research, I am interested in environmental advocacy and science communication. I also enjoy solo traveling, film photography, watercolor painting and pottery.
